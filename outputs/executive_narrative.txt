The Survival Strategy Framework - Synthetic Retention Demo run analyzed 7,500 records with an observed event rate of 39.52%. The regularized CoxPH model produced an apparent development-sample concordance index of 0.827; deterministic 5-fold validation produced a mean concordance of 0.827 (SD 0.007). The baseline high risk (top 25%) rule selected 1,875 records for same-cohort sensitivity testing. Among the illustrative improvement scenarios, 'Combined Retention Strategy' produced the largest modeled movement for the same 1,875-record target cohort: mean partial hazard declined by 52.86%, and modeled survival at 12 months increased by 18.79 percentage points. The run concluded PASS_WITH_REVIEW; proportional-hazards evidence was classified PASS_WITH_REVIEW. These outputs are model-based sensitivity evidence from demonstration data, not causal treatment effects, production retention forecasts, or realized financial outcomes.
